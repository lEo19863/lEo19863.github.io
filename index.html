Tapioca

Tapioca is a sarch extracted from the cassava plant. It is native to the north and central-west regions of Brazil. This starch plant is a staple food for millions of people in tropical countries, because it provides carbohydrate and is low in protein, vitamins and minerals. Recently, tapioca is used for tapioca balls, commonly referred to as boba or pearls when in bubble tea. They are edible translucent spheres, originated as a cheaper alternative to pearl sago in Southeast Asian cuisine. Often, we see pearls as black balls but by changing different ingredients, like sugar and seasoning, the pearls gain different color and texture. Tapioca are also used in jello and other Southeast Asian desserts.

Menu

My Recommendation

My recommended drink is the Milk Tea with Golden Bubbles.

At sugar level 50%.

Golden bubbles are made from tapioca and it is a special bubble served at Koi the. Unlike normal boba, it has a unique golden, transparent glow and a very chewy texture. They are quite small and easy to swallow.

The milk tea is a mix of Chinese tea and milk. it doesn't have a strong accent, but it is flavourful and bittersweet. It creates a harmony when it is topped off with golden bubbles because it adds more sweetness and texture.

HEYTEA

Place

HEYTEA originated in 2012, at Guandong, China. It all started with a unique inspirational idea of making a cheese tea. Through its success of making the first, original Cheezo tea, HEYTEAs' outlet spread through Asia like wildfire. Their teas are one of the best with tons of creative drinks!

Menu

My Recommendation

My favorite boba drink from this shop is the Milk Tea Bobashake.

At HEYTEA, some drinks are customizable, especially with ice cream. But I prefer it without customizing it too much because every single drink is carefully thought out and each one has an interesting twist other shops don't have.

The Milk Tea Bobashake is a milk tea with soft pearls coated with melted brown sugar. The fragrant chinese tea goes super well with the sweetness and roastiness of the pearls which are easy to chew. In addtion, unlike normal drinks with ice cubes, this bobashake has a creamy tea shake on top. It cools you down and adds texture in your drink! The ice in the shake is delicate, so there are no chunks in them.

CHICHA San Chen

Place

CHICHA San Chen is a store from Taiwan founded in 1998. Their values are to keep a high standard for tea drinkers while matching it with toppings like bubbles for an ultimate bubble tea. They offer high quality black and green tea, chosen and roasted by professional tea tasters and tea roasters. Customers with high expectation for tea would certainly love San Chen's drinks!

At CHICHA San Chen, you are able to create your own drink from the 5 stages:

  1. Selection of Tea
  2. Choosing Flavouring
  3. Choose Toppings
  4. Sugar and Ice Levels
  5. Complete!
Menu

My Recommendation

From this bubble tea shop, I recommend the Buble Milk Tea, 国王珍珠奶茶. It is a combination of black tea, cream and bubble. As expected, the black tea has a fragrant and roasty taste. The milk blends in well with tea, enhancing taste of the freshly brewed black tea with a sense of creaminess to it. The pearls from this shops is soft and chewy, reminding me of mochi. (A Japanese rice cake.) The pearls are also not very sticky, so they are easy to swallow and doesn't stick to your throat. I believe that San Chen's pearls are the softest!

LiHO

Place

LiHO TEA established in 2017 by Royal T GRoup. LiHO means 'How are you?' in Chinese dialect, bringing customers experience, feeling, adventure, along with fabulous drinks.

This bubble tea shop has a variety of drinks and toppings. They offer a unique series called Avocado Series for avocado fans. In addition, LiHO is the first shop to have CheezHO, a melt-in-your-mouth light cream with a hint of saltiness.

Since 2021, LiHO has updated their menu. Now, they offer two menus. The first menu has 6 categories and each with specific drinks. The other is a 'customize your own drink' menu.

My Recommendation

My recommendation from LiHO is the Earl Grey Milk Tea with Brown Sugar Pearls. Even though it is a milk tea,the distinctive taste of the earl grey remains. There is a good balace between the creaminess of the milk and fragrant taste of the tea. Furtheremore, the brown sugar pearls adds sweetness and toffee-like taste, creating the milk tea to become more rich and perfect for sweet lovers, like myself.

The pearls at LiHO are chewy and sweet. However, some people may find that the perals are more rubberly than chewy. So, people who prefer harder pearls or like to bite a lot, please try it out!
